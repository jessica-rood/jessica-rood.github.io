Working Papers

  1. NBER
    Beliefs, Attention, and Investments in Early Childhood
    Flávio Cunha, Snejana Nihtianova, Jessica Rood, and 1 more author
    2026
    Abs Bib HTML

    We evaluate the LENA Start Program, a group-based parent education intervention designed to increase early language exposure for children from low-income families, using a randomized controlled trial conducted with Alief Independent School District in Houston. Intent-to-treat analyses find significant effects on parental knowledge (0.50 SD) and beliefs (0.70 SD), conversational turns (0.30 SD), the broader home language environment (0.58 SD on StimQ), and child language development (0.28 SD on the LENA Developmental Snapshot). Heterogeneity analyses using causal forests find no systematic variation in treatment effects by observable characteristics. The pattern of effects suggests the intervention operates primarily through knowledge transmission rather than by building parental confidence or altering underlying preferences.

  1. Evolving Choices: Childcare and Maternal Labor Supply in a Dynamic Framework
    Jessica Rood
    2026
    Work in progress
    Abs Bib

    This paper develops and estimates a dynamic discrete choice model of maternal employment and childcare decisions using administrative data from New York City’s 3-K for All expansion. The central contribution is a methodology that separately identifies parental preferences over childcare arrangements from the access constraints that limit choice sets—a decomposition with significant implications for policy targeting. The estimated model is used to evaluate counterfactual policies including subsidy expansions and supply-side interventions.

  2. High Staff Turnover: The Systemic Barrier Undermining Early Childhood Education Quality Improvement Initiatives
    Jessica Rood and Ji-Hee Lee
    2026
    Work in progress
    Abs Bib

    This paper examines turnover in the early childhood education workforce using qualitative methods, asking how high staff turnover undermines the very initiatives designed to improve childcare quality. Drawing on 90+ interviews across three Texas-based quality improvement programs—Shared Services Alliances, the Early Educator Registered Apprenticeship Program, and the Pre-K Partnership Expansion Initiative—we identify a vicious cycle in which workforce instability hampers program implementation. Emerging themes include a professional-development paradox, threats to partnership viability, and a mismatch between program design and the realities of ECE careers.

  3. When Free Isn’t Enough: A Stated Choice Experiment to Understand Low Pre-K Enrollment
    Jessica Rood
    2026
    Work in progress
    Abs Bib

    This paper implements a stated choice experiment to identify parental preferences over early childhood program attributes, in partnership with Houston-area school districts. Despite substantial public investment, roughly 60% of eligible Houston families do not enroll in public Pre-K. By presenting hypothetical scenarios with experimentally varied attributes (cost, distance, hours, class size, setting), the study estimates the causal effect of each attribute on choice probability and converts these into willingness-to-pay measures. The work complements the revealed-preference analysis in the New York City paper.

  1. Evaluating an Eight-Week Baby Sign Language Program: An RCT Design
    Jessica Rood
    2026
    Active project
    Abs Bib

    An ongoing randomized controlled trial of an established 8-week baby sign language curriculum, examining effects on caregiver-child bonding, caregiver responsiveness, and child language and socio-emotional development. Currently in design and partner-recruitment phase, in collaboration with community partners in Houston, TX.
